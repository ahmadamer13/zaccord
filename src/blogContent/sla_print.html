Introduction

Learn the basics of Stereolithography, also known as SLA 3D printing. This introduction to Stereolithography (SLA) covers core principles needed to decide if it fits your application, plus key aspects of SLA 3D printing.

What Is Stereolithography?

SLA is an additive manufacturing process in the vat photopolymerization family (resin 3D printing). Three main processes belong here: SLA, DLP, and LCD. All use light to cure photopolymers, with these differences:

  • Stereolithography (SLA) uses a UV laser to selectively cure liquid resin
  • Digital Light Processing (DLP) uses a digital projector as a UV source to cure each layer
  • Liquid Crystal Display (LCD) uses an LCD module to mask specific light patterns

SLA is one of the most widely used vat photopolymerization technologies: a UV source selectively cures liquid resin layer by layer. SLA materials are light‑sensitive, thermosetting polymers processed in liquid form.

Patented in 1986, SLA was the first 3D printing technology. It remains cost‑effective when you need high accuracy or smooth surface parts. The best results come when designers leverage strengths and plan for limitations.

How SLA Works

1. The build platform is positioned in the resin tank one layer height below the surface.

2. A UV laser cures the first layer by selectively solidifying the photopolymer resin.

3. During curing, UV light activates polymerization of monomer chains in the liquid resin, forming solid, cross‑linked bonds.

4. The laser beam is guided along a predefined path via mirrors, scanning the cross‑section and curing most of the layer.

5. After printing, parts are not fully cured; post‑curing under UV improves mechanical properties.

Vat photopolymerization is irreversible; SLA parts cannot be returned to liquid. Heating SLA parts burns rather than melts them because resins are thermosets, unlike thermoplastics used in FDM.

SLA print parameters

Many parameters are fixed by manufacturers; common inputs are layer height and part orientation.

Layer Height

Typical layer heights range 25–100 microns. Lower layers capture curves better but increase time, cost, and risk; 100 microns suits many applications.

Build volume

Build volume depends on SLA machine type. Two main setups exist: top‑down and bottom‑up.

Bottom‑up printers place the light source below the resin tank and build parts upside‑down; after each layer the cured resin peels from the tank as the platform moves up. Top‑down printers place the light above and build face‑up; the platform moves down each layer.

SLA Characteristics

Key characteristics include the need for supports, warping risk, and interlayer bonding.

Supports

SLA often requires supports printed from the same material and removed after printing. Orientation determines support placement and quantity; avoid supports on visually critical surfaces.

Warping

A common accuracy issue is warping, similar to FDM. During curing, resin can shrink, creating internal stresses between new and previously cured layers that bend the part. Supports, careful orientation, and avoiding large thin flats help reduce warping.

Layer adhesion

SLA parts can achieve more isotropic properties than some other methods because post‑curing after printing further fuses layers. Curing continues even after the print completes.

Post‑processing

For best mechanical properties, post‑cure parts under intense UV in a curing chamber. This increases stiffness and heat resistance but can make parts more brittle. Post‑curing typically yields:

  • Nearly 2× higher tensile strength for standard resins after curing (e.g., ~65 MPa vs. ~38 MPa)
  • Higher heat deflection temperatures (e.g., ~58 °C vs. ~42 °C)
  • Lower elongation at break (e.g., ~6.2% vs. ~12%)

Summary

Advantages of SLA

  • High accuracy and fine detail possible.
  • Smooth surfaces ideal for visual prototypes.
  • Specialized resins available (clear, flexible, castable, etc.).
  • Efficient for rapid, visually appealing prototypes.

Disadvantages of SLA

  • SLA parts are often brittle and less suited for functional prototypes.
  • Az SLA alkatrészek mechanikai tulajdonságai és vizuális megjelenése idővel romlani fog, ha az alkatrészeket hosszasan napfény hatásának tesszük ki.
  • Ha szükség van tartószerkezetekre, az az SLA alkatrészeken vizuális nyomokat fog hagyni.

Az SLA 3D printing ökölszabályai:

  • Az SLA 3D printing akkor megfelelő számodra ha sima felületű és finom részletességű vizuális prototípusok előállítását szeretnéd.
  • Az SLA ideális kis méretű, fröccsöntéshez hasonló alkatrészek megfizethető áron történő gyártására.
  • Az SLA-val nyomtatott modellek nem alkalmasak mechanikailag megterhelő feladatok ellátására.
