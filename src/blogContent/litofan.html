Introduction

Learn the basics of lithophanes. A lithophane is a special art form from the past with a rich history. Today, anyone can make one from their own photos.

What is a lithophane?

Its magic comes from how the image appears. Lithophanes are thin, curved or flat rectangular panels with many fine details. These details only reveal themselves when light shines through from the back, forming the image. This unique reveal creates beautiful artwork with impressive detail. The way multiple elements work together to reveal the hidden image is what makes lithophanes so interesting.

How does a lithophane work?

Lithophanes are made from a thin material (historically porcelain) with varying thickness. Thicker areas block more light than thinner ones, so the transmitted light varies in intensity. This creates contrast that appears as different shades of gray. With fine, precise shaping, the result can display lifelike images.

3D‑printed lithophanes

For centuries, artists carved lithophanes from porcelain — a skill that required years of practice and extreme precision to achieve high detail.

With modern 3D printers, the possibilities have grown. 3D printing and lithophanes pair perfectly, bringing this classic art to the digital age. Since we store photos digitally, nearly any picture can be turned into a lithophane. Note that, like porcelain versions, 3D‑printed lithophanes are still two‑dimensional reliefs.
